# Product Builder Mobile (Android) — Take Home Task

**Goal:** Demonstrate your ability to take a loosely defined problem in the hospitality space and turn it into a validated, prototyped, and ready-for-real-solution — delivered as an Android application.

## Assignment

- **Identify a Problem in Hospitality** – Choose any real or imagined problem you've encountered while travelling, working in the industry, or observing gaps in the market. It can relate to guests, staff, check-in, check-out, operations, distribution, etc.

- **Define the Problem Clearly** – Who experiences it? Why is it worth solving? What makes it a good opportunity now?

- **Guide Us Through Your Discovery Process** – How would you validate this is a real and meaningful problem? What signals (qualitative or quantitative) would you use? What lightweight experiments would you run?

- **Prototype the Solution as an Android App** – Build an Android App prototype using Kotlin. No backend is required — use mock data to simulate real behaviour. The goal is to demonstrate your mobile product thinking and Android craft, not production infrastructure.
  - The prototype should run on an Android device or emulator and showcase the core user flow.
  - Use AI tooling (e.g. GitHub Copilot, Cursor, Lovable, Replit, Claude, etc.) to create a prototype or concept. Live commented demo of the solution. Highlight how AI helped you build faster, cheaper, or smarter.

- **Explain Your Prototyping & Delivery Strategy** – What would your MVP look like? How would you balance speed vs robustness? How would you ensure the solution is production-ready if taken beyond the prototype stage?

- **Plan for Adoption** – How would you encourage hotels (or guests, depending on your idea) to adopt your solution? What metrics would you use to measure success or engagement? How would you iterate based on feedback?

- **Work Without a PM or Designer** – Imagine you have no dedicated Product Manager or Designer. How would you stay aligned with business goals and ensure user experience quality? How would you gather feedback and prioritise?

## How to present the task

Prepare the presentation in the format you prefer — Miro board, Presentation, PDF, or similar. Send it one day prior to the session.

Also, share the code created in the prototype (GitHub link or similar repo).
